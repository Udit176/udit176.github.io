I am a graduate student pursuing a Master's degree in Autonomy at Purdue University, focusing on the theoretical and algorithmic underpinnings of offline reinforcement learning, coordinated multi-agent planning, and robust control for autonomous systems. My current research aims to develop safe and efficient decision-making strategies for networked robots and vehicles, drawing on principles from control theory, deep learning, and formal methods.

Prior to Purdue, I worked at the Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay, investigating stochastic 3D underwater navigation algorithms under the guidance of Professor Leena Vachhani. I also hold a Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering with a specialization in Mechatronics and Automation from the MIT Art, Design, and Technology University.
