About Me

Hello, I'm Udit Ekansh. I hold a B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering with a specialization in Mechatronics and Automation Engineering from MIT Art, Design, and Technology University, India (2023). Currently, I am pursuing my Master's in Autonomy at Purdue University, USA.

Before joining Purdue, I worked as a Research Assistant at the Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay, under the guidance of Professor Leena Vachhani. My research focused on developing autonomous navigation solutions for highly stochastic underwater environments.

My current research interests revolve around autonomy and its applications, particularly in offline reinforcement learning, autonomous and connected systems, and coordinated control strategies. I am passionate about bridging theoretical advancements with practical, real-world implementations.

Outside of my academic pursuits, I enjoy reading, playing chess, traveling, and geocaching. These hobbies allow me to explore new ideas, think strategically, and immerse myself in diverse cultures and environments.
