About

I am currently pursuing a Master’s degree in Autonomy at Purdue University’s Institute for Control, Optimization, and Networks (ICON) . My research centers on coordinated planning and control for autonomous and connected systems, with a strong emphasis on developing robust, data-driven algorithms to optimize multi-agent collaboration in dynamic, uncertain environments. By enabling machines to make better, more intelligent decisions, I aim to push the boundaries of autonomous technology and drive real-world impact in sectors such as mobility, logistics, and complex robotics.

Prior to joining Purdue, I worked as a Research Assistant at the Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay (IIT-B), where I designed and implemented navigation strategies for unmanned underwater vehicles operating in stochastic 3D environments. I also hold a Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering with a specialization in Mechatronics and Automation from the MIT Art, Design, and Technology University , which underpins my approach to merging advanced theoretical frameworks with practical system integration. Ultimately, I strive to create adaptable, resilient, and scalable autonomous systems that can transform human-machine collaboration across diverse industries.
